---
title: FAQ
description: Common questions about lawdit usage, safety, and prototype limits.
---

## Is lawdit a GDPR compliance product?

No. lawdit is a prelaunch prototype for accountable GDPR-relevant data discovery and review workflows. It does not provide legal advice and does not claim full GDPR compliance.

## Does a delete-candidate decision delete files?

No. Deletion remains simulated in P0. A delete-candidate decision records a human review outcome and audit event, but it does not delete external files.

## Why can I sign in but still see denied actions?

Sign-in identifies you. Workspace membership and permission boundaries decide what you can do. Denied actions are expected when an action is outside your role, Workspace, or prototype boundary.

## Why are snippets redacted?

lawdit is designed to support review without exposing raw sensitive content by default. Evidence snippets should be redacted so reviewers can understand context while reducing unnecessary personal-data exposure.

## Can I scan Google Drive files?

Only when the deployment has Google Picker public credentials configured and you select files or folders through the Picker. Drive selections store metadata, not persisted provider tokens. A scan needs a short-lived per-scan token.

## Can I scan any public URL?

No. Direct links must be HTTPS, must not contain embedded credentials, must not point to private or unsupported locations, and must pass file-size and format checks. Google Drive share-page URLs should be added through the Drive Picker flow.

## What happens when a file type is unsupported?

Unsupported or OCR-deferred files should be counted as warnings or deferred inputs. They should not silently disappear, and they should not cause lawdit to expose raw content.

## What should I do with unclear findings?

Use the visible review support. If the owner is wrong, reassign when allowed. If policy or risk is unclear, escalate to a supported queue or reviewer.

## What metrics should I check after a scan?

Check scanned files, flagged files, review backlog, high-risk count, owner routing, precision, recall, F1, reproducibility, throughput, and resource intensity.

## Does optional AI make decisions?

No. Optional AI can only support redacted context after deterministic processing and safety checks. It does not decide permissions, ownership, audit facts, legal conclusions, or deletion.

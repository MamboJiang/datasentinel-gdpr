---
title: Safety and Boundaries
description: Review the privacy, legal, deletion, permission, integration, and AI boundaries that keep the prototype accountable.
---

DataSentinel is designed to make boundaries visible. These boundaries help users understand what the product can do, what it refuses to do, and what still needs human judgement.

## Legal boundary

DataSentinel does not provide legal advice and does not claim full GDPR compliance. It presents evidence, policy-pack guidance, workflow state, audit records, and evaluation metrics so accountable people can review and decide.

## Deletion boundary

Deletion is simulated in P0.

A delete-candidate review decision records that a human believes cleanup should be considered. It does not delete external source files, Drive files, local files, or tenant data.

Denied real-deletion actions should remain visible in permission boundaries.

## Raw-content boundary

DataSentinel should not expose raw source bodies, raw extracted text, page images, unredacted personal data, provider secrets, provider tokens, refresh tokens, or client secrets in public payloads or user-facing screens.

The scan process can read supported source content during execution, but the durable product output should be metadata, redacted evidence, findings, metrics, and audit events.

## Source boundary

Prelaunch source input is limited to approved paths such as controlled sample sources, safe direct HTTPS links, Google Drive selected items, and host-allowed local sources.

The prototype does not add production Microsoft Graph, enterprise tenant, automatic deletion, SCIM, production RBAC, or broad directory integrations.

## Permission boundary

Users should see what is allowed and denied before they act. Authentication alone does not grant review authority, Workspace administration, source scanning, or deletion powers.

If a route or action is outside the current Workspace permission boundary, the UI should hide, disable, or clearly deny that destination or action.

## AI boundary

Deterministic scanning comes first. Optional AI support must be disabled unless configured, budget-guarded, redacted-evidence-only, and fail-closed when safety checks cannot pass.

AI support must not invent audit facts, decide ownership, decide permissions, claim legal conclusions, expose raw content, or instruct deletion.

## Safe use rules

- Do not paste raw sensitive content into feedback or support fields.
- Do not treat evidence summaries as legal advice.
- Confirm the source, evidence, owner, and permission boundary before reviewing.
- Use escalation when the correct owner or policy interpretation is unclear.
- Verify audit and evaluation output after important workflow actions.

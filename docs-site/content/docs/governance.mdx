---
title: Governance
description: Understand policy packs, organization context, permissions, review support, and change boundaries.
---

Governance is the layer that tells DataSentinel how to interpret evidence and route accountability. It should remain configurable and visible rather than hidden inside scanner code.

## Policy packs

Policy packs represent versioned review guidance. They can define evidence requirements, risk guidance, retention context, escalation paths, and reason requirements.

DataSentinel should preserve the active policy-pack version in scan, review, audit, and evaluation context when the contract provides it.

## Organization model

The organization model supports accountable routing. It can include direct owners, Master of Data fallbacks, privacy review queues, and escalation paths.

When a direct owner cannot be resolved, DataSentinel should route to a fallback or escalation path instead of leaving review-required findings silently unowned.

## Permission model

Permissions decide what each actor can view or do. The interface should expose both:

- Allowed actions.
- Denied actions with reasons.

Permission boundaries are especially important before review decisions, Workspace administration, source registration, scan start, and any simulated deletion path.

## Workspace administration

Workspace administrators can manage members, groups, invitations, and Workspace-level charts when their permission boundary allows it.

Expected administrator tasks include:

- Inspect members and pending invitations.
- Create, rename, edit, or delete allowed groups.
- Assign groups to members.
- Remove active members when doing so does not cause admin lockout.
- Generate invitation links with explicit group assignment.
- Review Workspace permission boundaries and management charts.

Workspace administration does not grant production tenant access, Microsoft Graph access, source-file deletion, legal conclusions, or hidden powers.

## Governance changes

When governance configuration changes, reviewers should be able to see the effect on permissions, routing, or review support before relying on it for decisions.

Changes should remain reversible and traceable through versioned policy and organization context.

## Optional AI support

Optional AI assistance is not the authority for governance. It may support context only after deterministic OCR and grep-style processing, and only with redacted evidence and active policy-pack context.

Owner decisions, permission decisions, audit facts, deletion instructions, and legal conclusions remain outside AI authority.

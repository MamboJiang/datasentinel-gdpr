---
title: Quick Start
description: Complete the first accountable discovery workflow from sign-in through audit and evaluation.
---

Use this path when you want to see DataSentinel working end to end.

## 1. Open the console

Open the product homepage and choose **Open dashboard**. If sign-in is required, the console shows a sign-in gate before it shows operational data.

If Google or GitHub sign-in is not configured for the current deployment, the sign-in gate shows provider setup status instead of exposing provider secrets or pretending that authentication succeeded.

## 2. Choose a Workspace

A Workspace is the boundary for members, groups, sources, findings, audit events, metrics, and evaluation state.

If you have no Workspace membership, use one of these options:

- Accept an invitation link from a Workspace administrator.
- Create a new Workspace if the deployment exposes that action to your account.

After a Workspace is selected, the sidebar shows only destinations allowed by your Workspace permission boundary.

## 3. Register or select a source

Open **Sources**. Depending on deployment setup, you may see:

- The controlled organizer sample source.
- A direct HTTPS file-link registration form.
- A Google Drive Picker option for selected files or folders.
- Host-allowed local source options in local preview environments.

Only scan-ready sources can start a full scan. A not-ready source should show a visible reason or receive a neutral rejection if you try to scan it.

## 4. Start a full scan

Start the scan from either:

- **Dashboard**, which targets the default ready sample source when available.
- **Sources**, which targets the selected source row.

The scan must target a specific source. DataSentinel records scan-start and scan-completion activity as audit-visible workflow events.

## 5. Follow progress on the Dashboard

The Dashboard groups scan, review, and pipeline status. Watch for:

- Scanned files and scanned volume.
- Flagged files and high-risk counts.
- Review backlog.
- Owner routing results.
- Pipeline stages such as inventory, extraction, signal detection, context/risk judgment, owner assignment, finding assembly, review support, audit recording, metrics, and evaluation.

Partial or recoverable warning states are expected for unsupported formats, OCR-deferred files, or unavailable integrations. They should be visible instead of silently hidden.

## 6. Review findings

Open **Findings** and choose a row. The finding detail page should show:

- Redacted evidence only.
- Detected signals and location context.
- Risk and retention context.
- Assigned owner or escalation path.
- Allowed and denied actions.
- Audit timeline.

Use the file review surface when available to focus on the relevant redacted evidence location.

## 7. Record a human decision

The review dialog shows decisions that are available to your current permission boundary. Depending on the finding and permissions, these can include:

- Delete candidate.
- Keep with reason.
- Correct false positive.
- Reassign owner.
- Escalate.

Each accepted decision needs the required reason and checklist acknowledgement. A keep-with-reason decision also needs a retention review date when the policy pack requires it.

## 8. Inspect audit and evaluation

Open **Audit Trail** to confirm the scan and review events. Open **Evaluation** to inspect quality and resource signals such as precision, recall, F1, reproducibility, throughput, model-call count, estimated paid-service cost, and rules traceability.

## Completion checklist

- A source was selected or registered.
- A full scan started against an explicit source.
- Findings were visible only with redacted evidence.
- A human decision was recorded with a reason.
- The audit trail showed the workflow event.
- Evaluation metrics stayed visible and did not imply legal compliance.
